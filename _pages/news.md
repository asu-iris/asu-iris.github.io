<div class="update-item">
    <div class="update-date">Jun. 2, 2025</div>
    <div class="update-content">
      <!-- <a  href="https://x.com/jinwanxin/status/1828130385806651428" target="_blank" role="button"> <i class="fa-brands fa-twitter fa-shake"></i> 𝕏-Twitter </a> -->
      <p>
        🚀🚀 Tracking unknown, highly dynamic objects in <strong>contact-rich scenes</strong>  — falling, bouncing, in-hand manipulation? Occlusion, blur, and impacts make it tough. Introducing 🔥🔥<a href="https://irislab.tech/TwinTrack-webpage/" target="_blank"><u>TwinTrack</u></a>🔥🔥: a physics-aware, <strong><u>real-time</u></strong> (>20 Hz) tracker that fuses <strong><u>Real2Sim</u></strong>  + <strong><u>Sim2Real</u></strong>, bridging vision and contact physics for reliable tracking. All GPU-accelerated!
      </p>
      <div class="video-container" style="position: relative; width: 100%; padding-top: 19%;">
        <video autoplay loop muted controls style="position: absolute; top: 0; left: 0; width: 100%; height: 100%;">
          <source src="/collections/research/manipulation/TwinTrack/video.mp4" type="video/mp4">
        </video>
      </div>
      <br />
      <ul style="font-size: 1rem; line-height: 1.6; padding-left:0">
        <li>🧠 <strong>Real2Sim</strong>: infers object geometry & physical properties from vision + contact cues</li>
        <li>👁️ <strong>Sim2Real</strong>: predicts motion via contact dynamics, fused with visual tracking</li>
        <li>💪 Robust to occlusion, blur, and impacts — no prior object model required</li>
        <li>🚀 GPU-accelerated and runs in real time at >20 Hz</li>
      </ul>
        Check out the <a href="https://irislab.tech/TwinTrack-webpage/" target="_blank"> webpage</a>,  <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.22882" target="_blank">preprint</a>, and <a href="https://youtu.be/UGn-kZzQsEg?si=mB18q3xX_reGZdQZ" target="_blank">method video</a>.

<div class="update-item">
    <div class="update-date">July 9 2024</div>
    <div class="update-content">
      <a  href="https://x.com/jinwanxin/status/1815216477748019473" target="_blank" role="button"> <i class="fa-brands fa-twitter fa-shake"></i> 𝕏-Twitter </a>
      <p>
        🤖 Robots may be good at inferring a task reward from human feedback, but how about inferring safety boundaries from human feedback? In many cases such as robot feeding and liquid pouring, specifying  user-comfortable safety constraints is more challenging  than  rewards. Our recent work, led by my PhD student <a href="https://zhi-xian-xie.github.io/" target="_blank">Zhixian Xie</a>,  shows that this is possible, and can actually be very human-effort efficient! Our method is called <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.04216" target="_blank">Safe MPC Alignment</a> (submitted to T-RO), enabling a robot to learn its control safety constraints with only a small handful of human online corrections!
      </p>
      Importantly, the Safe MPC Alignment is certifiable: providing an upper bound on the total number of human feedback in the case of successful learning of safety constraints, or declaring the misspecification of the hypothesis space, i.e., the true implicit safety constraint cannot be found within the specified hypothesis space.
